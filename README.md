# 📈 Financial Market Trading Performance & Risk Analysis

An interactive **Power BI dashboard** built using **Python, SQL, and Power BI** to analyze financial market trading performance, evaluate portfolio profitability, assess trading risk, and identify opportunities to improve investment decisions through data-driven insights.

---
# 🎯 Project Objective

Develop an end-to-end financial analytics solution to monitor trading performance, identify profitable stocks and sectors, evaluate trader performance, measure portfolio risk, and support strategic investment decisions.

## 🎯 Business Problem
- Despite executing thousands of stock trades, the company cannot clearly identify which stocks, sectors, or traders are generating profits and which are causing losses. Without this visibility, decision-making becomes difficult, reducing overall portfolio performance and profitability.
  
## ❓ Key Business Quetions
- Which stocks are the most & least profitable?
- Which sector is driving returns vs dragging performance?
- Which traders are performing best — and worst?
- How much is commission eating into profits?
- Are RSI signals being ignored?
- What is the volatility risk per stock?

# 🧹 Data Cleaning & Preprocessing

- Removed duplicate records

- Cleaned inconsistent stock ticker names
- Standardized trade type values
- Removed currency symbols

## 📈 Key Insights
- **MSFT** was the **most profitable stock**, contributing **32.46% of the profits among the top 5 stocks**. In contrast, **APL** contributed only **3.62%** of the **losses among the bottom 5 stocks**, showing that **MSFT** was the strongest performer while **APL** had the weakest performance.
- The **Healthcare sector** achieved the **highest return ratio (82.65%)**, making it the best-performing sector. In contrast, the **Consumer Cyclical** sector had the lowest return ratio **(49.66%)**, indicating weaker performance and a need to improve **investment strategies** in this sector.
- Among the **top 10 traders**, **TRD1042** achieved the highest profit **(14.58M)**, while **TRD1046** recorded the lowest profit **(9.72M)**. This shows a clear difference in trader performance and highlights opportunities to learn from top performers and improve the strategies of lower-performing traders.
- Broker commissions accounted for only **0.10%** of net profit, showing that transaction costs had very little impact on overall profitability. This means profits were mainly driven by trading performance rather than commission expenses.
- Most trades **(78.9%)** were made when the RSI was in the Neutral range, while only **11.0%** were made in Overbought conditions and **10.0%** in Oversold conditions. This suggests that RSI signals were not widely used in trading decisions and could be better utilized to improve trading performance.
- **MSFT (25.38%)** and **MICROSOFT (22.27%)** had the highest volatility, meaning their prices changed the most and carried higher risk. In contrast, **APL (3.50%)**, **JPM (2.60%)**, and **DIS (2.54%)** had the lowest volatility, making them more stable and lower-risk stocks.
